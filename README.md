# Sourced

**Medication safety you can verify. Every claim traces to a cited source.**

Sourced reviews a patient's medication list for dangerous interactions, adverse effects, and missing monitoring — and every single clinical claim in its output resolves to a specific FDA label field, interaction-database row, or adverse-event record. Its core principle is a hard line:

> **We never ask the model for a clinical fact.** Adverse effects and interactions are *retrieved from cited structured sources*, not generated by the model. The model only ranks, contextualizes, and explains evidence it was handed — and a second reviewer agent rejects any claim it cannot trace back to a source.

Built for **Built with Claude: Life Sciences** (Builder track).

## Why this matters

Medication-related harm is a leading preventable cause of hospitalization. The most dangerous moment is hospital discharge, when a heart patient goes home on 8–15 medications and interacting drugs are constantly started, stopped, and dose-adjusted. Pairwise interaction checkers (Lexicomp, Micromedex) answer *"do drug A and drug B interact?"* Sourced answers *"given THIS patient's labs, diagnoses, and allergies, what medication-safety issues are worth reviewing first — and prove it."*

## How it works

Retrieval and reasoning are cleanly separated. That separation is both the anti-hallucination moat and the reason no clinical query ever hits a model guardrail.

```
patient (ES/EN, messy) ──▶ INGEST ──▶ RETRIEVE ──▶ SYNTHESIZE ──▶ VERIFY ──▶ report
                          normalize    cited         Opus ranks    reviewer
                          to RxCUI     sources        + explains    rejects
                          (no LLM)     (no LLM)       (cited only)  untraceable
```

1. **Ingest + normalize** (`src/ingest`) — parses the patient context and resolves every drug to a canonical RxNorm RxCUI via RxNav. Handles Spanish names (`amiodarona` → amiodarone) and brand names (`Coumadin`); fails loudly on anything it can't resolve, never guesses.

2. **Deterministic retrieval** (`src/retrieve`) — for each drug and drug pair, queries **openFDA drug labels** (interactions, boxed warnings — the mechanism and authoritative text), **DDInter** (interaction severity), and **openFDA FAERS** (real-world adverse-event signal, clearly labeled as spontaneous-report, not causation). Plain code, no LLM. Each hit becomes an **evidence object**: `{claim_text, source_name, source_id, source_url, exact_field, quoted_text, retrieval_query, retrieved_at}`.

3. **Synthesize** (`src/synthesize`) — Claude Opus 4.8 receives *only* the patient context and the retrieved evidence objects. It emits a visible plan, then ranks and contextualizes findings against this patient's labs/diagnoses. Hard contract: it may not introduce any claim not present in the evidence; if it cannot cite an evidence id, it omits the claim.

4. **Verify** (`src/verify`) — a two-level reviewer. Level 1 (deterministic): every cited evidence id must resolve. Level 2 (adversarial, Opus): the reviewer re-reads each finding against the *verbatim quoted text* of its sources and rejects any assertion — a severity, a number, a monitoring instruction — that the source does not support. Only findings whose every claim traces to a source render.

Every report ships with a machine-readable **audit ledger** (`out/report.json`): every finding → its evidence → the exact query that produced it → timestamp. A reviewer can independently verify any single claim.

## Data sources

All public, all cited in output.

| Source | Role |
|---|---|
| [openFDA Drug Label](https://open.fda.gov/apis/drug/label/) | Interaction mechanism + boxed warnings (FDA-authoritative) |
| [DDInter](https://ddinter.scbdd.com/) | Drug-pair interaction severity (CC BY-NC) |
| [openFDA FAERS](https://open.fda.gov/apis/drug/event/) | Real-world adverse-event signal (not causation) |
| [RxNorm / RxNav](https://lhncbc.nlm.nih.gov/RxNav/) | Drug-name normalization to RxCUI |

Full provenance and honest per-source limitations: [SOURCES.md](SOURCES.md).

## Not a diagnosis tool

Sourced produces a **review packet for a licensed clinician or pharmacist**. It does not diagnose or prescribe. Patient context is processed locally; only public structured sources are queried outward. Demo patient is synthetic (no PHI), reviewed by a licensed pharmacist for plausibility.
